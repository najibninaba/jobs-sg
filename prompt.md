# AI Exposure of the Singapore Job Market

This document contains structured data on Singapore occupations from the Ministry of Manpower wage survey, enriched with SSOC occupation metadata and scored for AI exposure on a 0-10 scale. Use it for grounded analysis and discussion of how AI may reshape the Singapore labour market.

GitHub: https://github.com/najibninaba/jobs-sg

## Scoring methodology

Each occupation is scored on a single **AI Exposure** axis from 0 to 10, measuring how much AI is likely to reshape the occupation. The score considers both direct automation (AI doing the work) and indirect effects (AI making workers much more productive, reducing labour demand).

A key heuristic is whether the occupation's work product is fundamentally digital. If the work can largely be done from a computer — writing, coding, analysing, designing, documenting, communicating — exposure is inherently high. Occupations requiring physical presence, manual skill, or real-time human interaction in the physical world have more natural protection.

Calibration anchors:
- 0-1 Minimal: cleaners, labourers, refuse collectors
- 2-3 Low: hawker stall assistants, hairdressers, bus drivers
- 4-5 Moderate: staff nurses, preschool teachers, property agents
- 6-7 High: accountants, HR executives, sales managers, journalists
- 8-9 Very high: software developers, graphic designers, translators, data analysts
- 10 Maximum: data entry clerks, routine telemarketing-style work
